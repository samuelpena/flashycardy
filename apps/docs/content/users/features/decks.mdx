# Decks

A **deck** is a collection of flashcards grouped around one topic — for example, "French verbs" or "AWS certification notes."

## When to use decks

Create a separate deck for each subject or exam you are preparing for. Keeping topics separate makes it easier to study one area at a time and track progress per subject.

## What you see on the Dashboard

The **Dashboard** lists all your decks. Each deck card shows:

- **Name** and **Description**
- **Card count** — how many flashcards are in the deck
- **Last updated** date
- **Study session count** — how many times you have completed a study session for that deck (when available)

Click a deck card to open it. Use the sort menu to order decks by **Last updated**, **A → Z**, or **Z → A**.

## Create a deck

1. Click **New Deck** on the Dashboard.
2. Enter a **Name** (required) and optional **Description**.
3. Click **Create deck**.

On the **Free plan**, you can create up to **3 decks**. If you reach the limit, the **New Deck** button opens a dialog explaining the limit and linking to **View Plans**. See [Deck limit reached](/users/troubleshooting/deck-limit-reached).

{/* screenshot: deck-limit-dialog */}

## Edit a deck

1. On the Dashboard or inside the deck detail page, click the **Edit** icon on the deck.
2. Change the **Name** or **Description**.
3. Click **Save changes**.

## Delete a deck

1. Click the **Delete** icon on the deck.
2. Confirm the deletion — the dialog shows the deck name and how many cards will be removed.
3. Click **Delete deck**.

Deleting a deck permanently removes all cards inside it. This cannot be undone.

## Expected outcome

After creating a deck, it appears on your Dashboard and is ready for cards. After editing, the updated name and description show immediately. After deleting, the deck disappears from your library.
